# Tenets

These are the principles that resolve real product tradeoffs at Cat Food Center. When two options conflict, the higher tenet wins. A tenet that everyone agrees with without hesitation is not useful — these are meant to be opinionated.

---

## 1. The score must be trustworthy before it is comprehensive

An inaccurate score on 10,000 products is worse than an accurate score on 1,000. When we lack the data to score confidently, we say so explicitly and show a partial-data indicator rather than filling gaps with assumptions. Coverage grows over time; credibility, once lost, does not come back.

*This tenet resolves: shipping a larger catalog quickly vs. shipping a smaller, verified one. We ship smaller and verified.*

---

## 2. Science beats marketing

We score what matters for cat health — animal protein dominance, taurine sufficiency, additive risk — not what sounds good on a bag. "Grain-free," "natural," "holistic," and "premium" are marketing terms we ignore entirely unless they correlate with measurable nutritional or safety outcomes. A product with impressive marketing but propylene glycol in the ingredient list scores Bad.

*This tenet resolves: pressure to treat "premium" branding as a positive signal. We don't.*

---

## 3. No revenue path touches the score

Cat Food Center is useful only if owners trust it. No advertiser, sponsor, partner, or brand may influence a product's CFC Score or additive flags. If we ever take money, it is visually separated from ratings and has zero effect on the algorithm. This is a hard rule, not a preference we revisit when revenue is tight.

*This tenet resolves: business model discussions that suggest "featured" or "boosted" placements in results. We don't do it.*

---

## 4. The verdict in one second, the reasoning behind it

The score and band are the headline. Every design decision on the product page prioritizes showing the number and verdict immediately, before anything else loads or scrolls into view. Drilling into the reasoning (ingredients, additive sources, nutrition math) is always available but never required to get a useful answer.

*This tenet resolves: whether to show more data vs. a cleaner, faster verdict. We default to the faster verdict.*

---

## 5. Inform, never prescribe

Cat Food Center tells owners what is in a product and what the evidence says about it. It does not tell them what to feed their cat. We flag propylene glycol because the FDA prohibits it in cat food. We do not say "switch to Brand X." Owners with cats that have medical conditions are explicitly directed to their veterinarian, not to our recommendations.

*This tenet resolves: whether to add personalized diet recommendations or "best for your cat" features. We don't, in v1.*

---

## 6. Mobile is the real use case

Ratings that can only be consulted at a desk don't change purchasing decisions. Every feature is designed to work under store conditions: one hand, ambient noise, urgency, low patience. If a feature is only comfortable on a large screen, it is a nice-to-have. If it's only comfortable on a phone, it ships first.

*This tenet resolves: feature prioritization between desktop-only capabilities and mobile-first flows. Mobile ships first.*
